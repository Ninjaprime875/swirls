Endangered


Since the early 20th century, tigers populations have lost 93% of their global range. They are extinct in the wild in West and Central Asia and in large areas of China. Tigers are listed as endangered on the IUCN Red List.


However, the global wild tiger population is estimated to be around 5,574, a significant increase from an all-time low of 3,200 in 2010, according to the Global Tiger Forum.


Major threats to tigers populations include habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict. There are national conservation efforts, such as anti-poaching patrols, to decrease poaching and promote the coexistence between tigers and communities.
